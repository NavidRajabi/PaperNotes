Summarization-based Video Caption via Deep Neural Networks

Flowchart of their proposed framework:

video -> deep cnn(cnn features) -> rnn(lstm) -> summarization process(initial graph, final candidates).

Target function: \[ \theta^* = argmax_{\theta} \sum_{(F,S)} log p(S|F; \theta)\]

where \[S\] represents a sequence of words whose length is unbounded.

The formula above can be expressed as: \[ p(S|F)=\sum_{t=0}^N log p(S_{W_t}|F,S_()W_1),...,S_{W_{t-1}}\]

where \[N\] is the total number of words in sentence \[S\] and \[S_{W_t}\] representes the \[i\]-th word in sentence \[S\].

They use summarization process to reduce outliers in sentences sequence that share little similarity with others, the LexRank score is defined as follows: \[ LR(u)=(1-d)\sum_{u \in adj[u]} \frac{LR(v)}{deg(v)} + \frac{d}{N}\]

Initial LexRank score is \[d/N\].
